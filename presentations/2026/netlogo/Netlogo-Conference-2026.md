# Modeling Religion and Political Polarization in NetLogo

Political polarization and cultural division have become increasingly prominent in recent years, prompting researchers to explore how these dynamics affect social systems. One area of interest is the role of church communities, which, while united by shared theological beliefs, also serve as social networks where political and cultural disagreements can impact participation and cohesion.

In this presentation, we will explore how NetLogo can be used to model the interactions between church communities and political polarization. We will discuss the design of an agent-based model that simulates individuals within a church community, their political beliefs, and how these beliefs influence their participation in church activities.

This model will allow us to examine scenarios such as:

- How political polarization within a church community affects attendance and engagement.
- The impact of social influence and peer pressure on individuals' political beliefs and church participation.
- The potential for church communities to either mitigate or exacerbate political polarization.

We consider three aspects for this model: the sense of belonging, the beliefs, and the behavior of the agents. The sense of belonging is influenced by the political beliefs of the agents and their interactions with others in the church community. The beliefs of the agents are shaped by their social environment, including the influence of other members and external political factors. The behavior of the agents, such as attending church services or participating in church activities, is affected by their sense of belonging and their beliefs.

Our model is part of a larger research project that aims to understand the complex interplay between religion and politics in contemporary society. By using NetLogo, we can visualize and analyze these dynamics in a way that provides insights into how church communities navigate political differences and maintain social cohesion.
